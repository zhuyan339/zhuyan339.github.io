Yan Zhu

Behavior Analyst | Data Scientist | Behavioral Intervention Systems Researcher

I work at the intersection of applied behavior analysis, data science, and behavioral intervention systems. My interests focus on how evidence-based behavioral interventions can be implemented with higher quality, stronger treatment fidelity, and better long-term sustainability in real-world clinical and educational settings.

Implementation Quality & Treatment Fidelity

My primary research interest is how behavioral interventions are implemented in real-world service systems. I am especially interested in treatment fidelity, procedural integrity, implementation drift, supervision structures, and the conditions that support consistent, high-quality intervention delivery.

Supervision, Training & Clinical Decision Systems

I am interested in how staff training, supervision systems, feedback loops, and clinical decision-making processes influence intervention quality. My work explores how practitioners can be better supported through structured training, data-informed feedback, and scalable implementation supports.

Scalable Behavioral Intervention Systems

I am interested in designing behavioral systems that support durable behavior change across naturalistic settings. This includes generalization, maintenance, caregiver involvement, technology-enabled service delivery, and data systems that help translate evidence-based practices into sustainable real-world implementation.

Research Preparation

My research preparation combines psychological research training, applied behavior analysis, evidence synthesis, and data-informed practice. Although my current work is pre-publication, my training and experience have shaped a clear research trajectory focused on behavioral intervention systems and implementation quality.

Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis Research

Contributed to an early-stage collaborative systematic review and meta-analysis project examining associations between sexual violence experiences and shame-related outcomes. The project involved psychological literature review, study organization, evidence synthesis, and conference abstract preparation.

I present this experience as research training rather than as a publication, as the final public article lists a revised author group.

Literature Review & Evidence Synthesis

Experience reviewing psychological and behavioral science literature, organizing study-level information, identifying methodological limitations, and connecting empirical findings to applied research questions.

Research Methods & Data Interpretation

Training and interest in research design, effect size interpretation, data visualization, research quality evaluation, and translating empirical findings into applied behavioral systems.

Behavioral Science Research Alignment
